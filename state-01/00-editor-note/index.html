The Forces Have Moved

Why STATE exists, and why this issue. Patterns are frozen resolutions of forces — and the forces just moved. Two days in Miami, one through-line.

Patterns are frozen resolutions of forces. That’s Christopher Alexander. When the forces move, every pattern you trusted becomes, for a little while, a question again.

The forces just moved. That’s what these two days in Miami felt like — a room full of people who build AI software for a living, reporting back from their own practice on which patterns had cracked and which ones hadn’t refrozen. The most credible thing anyone did on that stage was change their mind out loud, and plenty of them did.

STATE is a dispatch. First-person, reportage, not declarative. When we’ve learned something general enough to be declarative, it goes in SURFACE, RUNTIME, HARNESS, LOCAL, WIRE — the other issues in this publication, each of which cashes in on a specific thread from these two days.

This issue has two long dispatches — Day One and Day Two — plus a closing on the question that was underneath everything: what is an agent, actually? Read them in order if you want the arc. Skip to the closing if you want the shape.

The patterns haven’t refrozen. That’s the whole point.

— Will Schenk · The Focus.AI
